## Manuscript Summary

This paper presents and discusses the AI-PRO (AI-Powered integrated Reservoir, flow, and Process Optimization) framework, which addresses two interconnected challenges: the computational bottleneck in integrated production optimization for mature petroleum fields, and the growing need to train engineers fluent in both domain-specific engineering and applied artificial intelligence.

We discuss how physics-informed neural networks, deep surrogate models, and reinforcement learning agents can be combined with open-source simulation tools (NeqSim, OPM Flow) to enable system-level production optimization at the speed required for real-time operational deployment. We introduce an equipment utilization methodology that translates integrated simulation outputs into actionable bottleneck maps. Beyond its industrial significance, we describe how the project integrates research outputs directly into engineering curricula at NTNU through a new course module, industry-connected MSc thesis supervision, and an open-source software library designed as both a research and teaching resource.

## Relevance to Journal Scope

The manuscript falls squarely within the scope of Computers & Chemical Engineering, addressing:
- **Modeling, simulation, and optimization** of integrated production systems
- **Artificial intelligence and machine learning** applications in chemical and process engineering
- **Cyberinfrastructure and informatics** through digital twin architectures and open-source tools
- **Process operations and planning** through equipment utilization metrics and bottleneck identification

The educational dimension addresses the journal's interest in how computational methods are taught and adopted by the engineering community.
