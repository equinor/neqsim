# Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

Please find enclosed our manuscript entitled **"An Open-Source Thermodynamic Engine for Oil and Gas Process Digital Twins: Architecture, Capabilities, and Future Directions"** for consideration for publication in *Computers & Chemical Engineering*.

## Why this paper matters

Digital twins are transforming operations across the oil and gas industry, yet most implementations rely on proprietary commercial simulation software that creates vendor lock-in, limits reproducibility, and hinders the integration of modern AI technologies. This paper presents a comprehensive architecture for building process digital twins using an open-source thermodynamic and process simulation platform, covering the full stack from equation-of-state calculations through cloud-deployed live operation.

## Key contributions

1. **Complete digital twin architecture**: We describe a platform with 60+ equation-of-state implementations, 33 process equipment packages, transient simulation, and a five-step deployment pipeline from offline model building through cloud-hosted operation — the first systematic presentation of such a complete open-source digital twin stack for oil and gas.

2. **Novel instrumentation framework**: The tag-based instrument role classification (INPUT/OUTPUT/BENCHMARK) provides a structured methodology for connecting process models to plant historians, automating the model-plant comparison that is central to digital twin operation.

3. **Systematic platform comparison**: We provide the first head-to-head comparison of digital twin capabilities across open-source and commercial platforms (Aspen HYSYS, UniSim Design, DWSIM, COCO) across nine capability dimensions.

4. **AI integration frontiers**: We discuss how Model Context Protocol (MCP) integration and multi-agent LLM frameworks create new pathways for automated digital twin development and maintenance — capabilities with no equivalent in existing commercial or open-source platforms.

## Scope and audience

The paper addresses the intersection of process simulation, industrial digitalization, and open-source software — topics of strong interest to the CACE readership. The work has direct practical relevance: the platform is deployed in production digital twin applications on the Norwegian Continental Shelf and is freely available under an open-source license.
